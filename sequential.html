Sequential Theory

Sequential circuits have memory — their output depends not just on current inputs but also on the previous state. A clock signal synchronises when state changes occur.

Clock and Memory

Sequential circuits respond on rising or falling edges of the clock.

SR Flip-Flop

Set-Reset flip-flop. S sets Q to 1; R resets Q to 0.

Invalid state: S = 1, R = 1

D Flip-Flop

The output Q follows input D on the next clock edge.

Q(next) = D

T Flip-Flop

When T=1, the output toggles on every clock edge.

Q(next) = T XOR Q

JK Flip-Flop

Improves SR flip-flop by removing the invalid state. J=1 and K=1 causes toggle.

Q(next) = JQ̄ + K̄Q

Master-Slave JK

Uses two JK flip-flops in series to avoid race around condition.

  • Clock HIGH: Master captures input, Slave stays disabled.
  • Clock LOW: Master locks, Slave copies master output.

This ensures only one output change per clock cycle.
