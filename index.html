Introduction

Digital Electronics deals with signals that have only two states: 0 (LOW) and 1 (HIGH). These binary values form the basis of modern computers, smartphones, and digital devices.

All information inside a digital system — numbers, text, images, and sound — is ultimately stored and processed as combinations of 0s and 1s. Understanding how these bits are manipulated using logic gates is the first step toward understanding computers and embedded systems.

Why Digital Electronics?

  • Foundation of Computers: CPUs are built from billions of logic gates and flip-flops.
  • Microprocessors and Microcontrollers: Used in Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and embedded systems.
  • Communication Systems: Digital signals are more noise-resistant than analog.
  • Storage: RAM, SSDs, and hard drives store data as binary bits.
  • Signal Processing: Audio, video, and image processing rely on digital logic.
  • Counters and Timers: Found in digital clocks and CPU stages.
  • Universal Design: NAND and NOR gates alone can implement any logic function.
